Your Time Away From Time

The Bar is deeper than it seems from the outside and so filled with smoke it makes your eyes water. There are occasionally the rare few people here: locals from nearby ghost towns, wanderers who blow through like tumbleweeds.

But here, now, it's quiet. Supertramp softly plays from a dimly glowing jukebox in a rear corner that feels vertiginously far away. In the dim nicotine haze, the diffused halos of the lights, the Bartender stands with his back to the door.

His hands are reflected in the mirror behind the bar, and they'd seem to match the rest of him: creased, well-worn.
